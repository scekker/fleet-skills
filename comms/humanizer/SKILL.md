# Humanizer

Rewrites AI-generated text to read as natural human prose. Two passes: rewrite, then audit.

## When to Use Science Patterns

If the text is a research paper, grant narrative, lab report, or scientific one-pager: load `references/science-patterns.md` in addition to the base patterns below.

For all other text: base patterns only.

## Base Patterns (25 — from Wikipedia Signs of AI Writing)

Apply all that are relevant. Skip patterns that don't appear in the text.

### Group 1: Inflated Claims
1. **Significance inflation** — "pivotal moment," "transformative potential," "groundbreaking" → state the actual fact
2. **Notability name-dropping** — "cited in NYT, BBC, FT" → "In a 2024 NYT interview, she argued..."
3. **Superficial -ing analyses** — "symbolizing... reflecting... showcasing..." → remove or replace with sourced facts
4. **Promotional language** — "nestled within the breathtaking region" → "is a town in the Gonder region"
5. **Vague attributions** — "Experts believe it plays a crucial role" → "according to a 2019 survey by..."
6. **Formulaic challenges** — "Despite challenges... continues to thrive" → specific facts about actual challenges

### Group 2: Phrasing Patterns
7. **AI vocabulary** — "Additionally... testament... landscape... showcasing... delve... robust... foster... underscore" → cut or use plain synonyms
8. **Copula avoidance** — "serves as... features... boasts... functions as" → "is... has"
9. **Negative parallelisms** — "It's not just X, it's Y" → state the point directly
10. **Rule of three** — "innovation, inspiration, and insights" → use the natural number of items
11. **Synonym cycling** — "protagonist... main character... central figure... hero" → pick one and repeat it
12. **False ranges** — "from the Big Bang to dark matter" → list topics directly

### Group 3: Formatting
13. **Em dash overuse** — "institutions—not the people—yet this continues—" → commas or periods
14. **Boldface overuse** — bold every third noun phrase → bold only what genuinely needs emphasis
15. **Inline-header lists** — "Performance: Performance improved" → convert to prose
16. **Title Case Headings** — "Strategic Negotiations And Partnerships" → "Strategic negotiations and partnerships"
17. **Emojis in body text** — "🚀 Launch Phase: 💡 Key Insight:" → remove
18. **Curly quotes** — "the project" → "the project" (straight quotes in plain text)
25. **Hyphenated word pairs** — "cross-functional, data-driven, client-facing" → drop hyphens on common pairs

### Group 4: Chatbot Artifacts
19. **Chatbot artifacts** — "I hope this helps! Let me know if..." → remove entirely
20. **Cutoff disclaimers** — "While details are limited in available sources..." → find sources or remove
21. **Sycophantic tone** — "Great question! You're absolutely right!" → respond directly

### Group 5: Filler and Hedging
22. **Filler phrases** — "In order to" → "To"; "Due to the fact that" → "Because"
23. **Excessive hedging** — "could potentially possibly" → "may"
24. **Generic conclusions** — "The future looks bright" → specific plans or facts

## Process

### Pass 1 — Rewrite
Apply all relevant patterns. Goal: accurate, direct prose. Do not soften or inflate findings; report what the data actually shows.

Rules:
- Preserve all numbers, statistics, citations, and named entities exactly
- Use active voice where natural; passive is fine in Methods
- Past tense for completed work
- Do not add content — only rewrite what is there
- Tables stay as tables; do not flatten data into prose

### Pass 2 — Audit
After rewriting, read the output and ask: "Does any sentence still sound like it was generated by a language model?" Flag and fix:
- Any remaining AI vocabulary (delve, robust, foster, underscore, testament, landscape, pivotal)
- Any sentence that makes a claim without a specific fact behind it
- Any conclusion that is vaguer than the evidence warrants
- Any place where "Taken together" or "Collectively" appears

Fix flagged items. Output the final version.

## Notes
- For documents longer than ~2000 words, process section by section to maintain quality
- The audit pass often catches "Taken together," "Collectively," and "These results demonstrate" — treat all three as automatic flags
- Preserve section headers and document structure; only rewrite body text and inline formatting
